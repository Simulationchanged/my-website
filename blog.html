A More Creative Brain


Written by James Clear

Date: 30.October.2023



The main theme of the article is the importance of the creative process in problem-solving. Creativity is described as the ability to forge new connections between old ideas. This creative process can be cultivated in five steps, as demonstrated by the example of Frederic Eugene Ives, who developed a groundbreaking printing technique. This method, called "halftone printing," fundamentally changed the printing industry. His journey mirrors the five stages described by James Webb Young:

  • gather new information
  • mentally process this information
  • step back from the problem
  • wait for an insight or solution
  • refine the idea based on feedback

In summary, creativity is about linking existing concepts in innovative ways.
